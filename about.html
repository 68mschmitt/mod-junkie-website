Approach

How the shop handles work.

  1. Listen to the symptom or goal.

    Start with what the owner is experiencing, what changed, and what the car is being used for.

  2. Inspect before recommending parts.

    Diagnostics and inspection come before assumptions, especially on electrical, drivability, and performance concerns.

  3. Explain findings in plain language.

    Repairs and upgrades should make sense before money is spent.

  4. Get approval before work begins.

    No work begins without approval. You stay informed before parts are ordered or additional work is performed.
